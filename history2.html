Chapter 6: The First War of Independence (1857-1858)

Causes of the Revolt

Political Causes

  • Annexation Policies (Doctrine of Lapse)
  • Annexation of Oudh and loss of local aristocracy

Economic Causes

  • Destruction of Indian industries
  • High taxes, land revenue exploitation
  • Land revenue system like Permanent Settlement

Social and Religious Causes

  • Interference in Hindu and Muslim religious practices
  • British social reforms like Widow Remarriage Act and Sati abolition

Military Causes

  • Use of Enfield rifle cartridges and the greased fat rumor
  • Poor pay and harsh treatment of sepoys

Immediate Cause

  • Mutiny at Meerut, May 10, 1857
Course of the Revolt

Spread of Revolt

  • Delhi, Kanpur, Lucknow, Jhansi, Bareilly, and others

Key Figures

  • Bahadur Shah Zafar, Nana Saheb, Tantia Tope, Rani Lakshmibai, Begum Hazrat Mahal

Important Events

  • Delhi: Bahadur Shah Zafar declared leader, British recapture Delhi in September 1857
  • Kanpur: Nana Sahebâ€™s resistance, eventual British retake in June 1857
  • Jhansi: Rani Lakshmibai's leadership, death in battle
Consequences of the Revolt
  • End of East India Company Rule: Direct British government control (British Raj) from 1858
  • British Reforms: Military and administrative changes, 'divide and rule' strategy
  • Indian Nationalism: Revolt marked the beginning of Indian resistance to colonial rule

Chapter 7: The Making of the National Movement: 1870s-1947

Early Nationalism (Late 19th Century)

Foundation of Indian National Congress

  • Founded in 1885
  • Moderates like Dadabhai Naoroji and Surendranath Banerjee

Moderate Phase (1885-1905)

  • Belief in petitions, reforms within British framework
  • Dadabhai Naoroji's Drain of Wealth Theory
Rise of Extremist Nationalism (1905-1919)

Partition of Bengal (1905)

  • British policy of 'divide and rule', protests, and boycotts

Swadeshi Movement (1905-1908)

  • Promotion of Indian goods, boycott of British products
World War I and Aftermath

Indian Contribution in WWI

  • India's support to British in WWI in exchange for reforms

Rowlatt Act (1919)

  • Repressive measures, mass protests, Jallianwala Bagh massacre
Gandhi's Leadership and Non-Cooperation Movement

Gandhi's Arrival (1915)

  • Return from South Africa, principles of Ahimsa (non-violence) and Satyagraha (truth-force)

Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922)

  • Boycott of British goods, non-violent resistance
  • Chauri Chaura incident and suspension of movement
